KANNUR

Historic

Kannur is a district located in the northern part of the Indian state of Kerala. The district is known for its rich cultural heritage, pristine beaches, and historical significance.
The town of Kannur is famous for its traditional folk art forms, including Theyyam, which is a unique ritualistic art form that is performed in temples across the district.
Kannur is also home to a number of historic forts and buildings, including the St. Angelo Fort, which was built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and is a major tourist attraction.
The district is also home to a number of beautiful beaches, including Payyambalam Beach and Meenkunnu Beach, which are popular spots for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
Other popular tourist attractions in Kannur include the Arakkal Museum, which showcases the history of the Arakkal family, who were the only Muslim royal family in Kerala, and the Muzhappilangad Beach, which is the only drive-in beach in Kerala.

Major Attractions
  1. St. Angelo Fort: This historic fort, built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, is a major tourist attraction in Kannur and offers a glimpse into the region's colonial past.
  2. Theyyam: This unique ritualistic folk art form is performed in temples across Kannur and is a must-see cultural experience for anyone visiting the region.
  3. Arakkal Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Arakkal family, who were the only Muslim royal family in Kerala and played a significant role in the region's history.
  4. Muzhappilangad Beach: This beautiful beach is the only drive-in beach in Kerala and is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
  5. Payyambalam Beach: This scenic beach is known for its golden sand and clear water and is a popular spot for swimming and picnics.
  6. Parassinikadavu Temple: This ancient temple dedicated to Lord Muthappan is a popular pilgrimage site in Kannur and is known for its unique architecture and daily rituals.
  7. Kavvayi Backwaters: This serene network of lagoons and canals is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and bird-watching.
