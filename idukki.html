IDUKKI

Scenic

Idukki is a district located in the Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its scenic beauty, wildlife, and rich biodiversity. The district is situated in the Western Ghats mountain range, and the majority of the land is covered by forests, hills, and valleys.
Idukki is home to several wildlife sanctuaries and national parks, including the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, the Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary, and the Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary. These protected areas are home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, leopards, and rare bird species.
The district is also known for its beautiful dams, including the Idukki Arch Dam, which is one of the highest arch dams in Asia. These dams are an important source of hydroelectric power for the state and offer stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Idukki is also a popular destination for trekking and adventure activities, with several scenic trails and camping sites available for visitors.
The district is home to several hill stations, including Munnar, which is known for its beautiful tea plantations, and Vagamon, which offers stunning views of the Western Ghats.
Overall, Idukki is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers, offering visitors a unique experience of Kerala's natural beauty and wildlife.

Major Attractions
  1. Munnar: This hill station is located in the Western Ghats and is known for its lush green tea plantations, misty hills, and scenic waterfalls.
  2. Idukki Arch Dam: This arch dam is one of the highest arch dams in Asia and is built across the Periyar River. It offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  3. Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary: This wildlife sanctuary is situated in the Cardamom Hills and is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, leopards, and rare bird species.
  4. Mattupetty Dam: This gravity dam is built across the Mattupetty River and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  5. Anamudi Peak: This peak is the highest peak in South India and is located in the Western Ghats. It is a popular destination for trekking and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  6. Thekkady: This town is located on the border of Kerala and Tamil Nadu and is known for its spice plantations, wildlife sanctuaries, and adventure activities like trekking, boating, and jungle safaris.
  7. Keezharkuthu Falls: Also known as Rainbow Falls, this waterfall is situated in the Western Ghats and offers a stunning view of a rainbow over the falls.
