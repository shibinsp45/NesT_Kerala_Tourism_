ERNAKULAM

Cosmopolitan

Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a vibrant coastal city located in the state of Kerala, India. Situated on the southwest coast of India, Kochi has a rich history as a major trading center for spices and other commodities.
Kochi is known for its beautiful backwaters, beaches, historic monuments, and cultural diversity. The city has a thriving tourism industry and attracts visitors from all over the world. Some of the major attractions of Kochi include the historic Fort Kochi, which has a unique blend of Dutch, Portuguese, and British architecture.
The Chinese fishing nets at Fort Kochi are also a popular tourist attraction. Kochi is also known for its beautiful backwaters, which can be explored by taking a boat ride or a houseboat cruise. The city is also home to several beautiful beaches, such as Cherai Beach, Fort Kochi Beach, and Vypeen Beach. Other popular attractions in Kochi include the Jewish Synagogue, St. Francis Church, the Dutch Palace, and the Hill Palace Museum.
The city also has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its traditional dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam.
Overall, Kochi is a must-visit destination in Kerala, offering visitors a unique experience of Kerala's culture, history, and natural beauty.

Major Attractions
  1. Fort Kochi: This historic neighborhood in Kochi is known for its colonial architecture, charming streets, and cultural heritage. It is also home to popular tourist attractions like St. Francis Church, Chinese fishing nets, and the Dutch Palace.
  2. Marine Drive: This scenic promenade along the backwaters of Kochi offers a beautiful view of the city skyline and is a popular spot for evening walks, boat rides, and street food.
  3. Hill Palace Museum: This museum is located in a former royal palace and showcases the rich history and culture of Kerala. It has a vast collection of artifacts, paintings, and sculptures.
  4. Bolgatty Palace: This historic palace-turned-hotel is situated on a lush green island in the middle of Kochi backwaters. It offers stunning views of the surrounding water and is a popular spot for leisure activities like golfing and boating.
  5. Jewish Synagogue: This 400-year-old synagogue is located in the heart of Kochi's Jew Town and is one of the oldest in India. It has a beautiful interior with ornate chandeliers, hand-painted tiles, and ancient scrolls.
  6. Kerala Folklore Museum: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of Kerala and has a vast collection of artifacts, costumes, and musical instruments.
  7. Shopping: Ernakulam has several shopping destinations, including the famous Lulu Mall, one of the largest malls in India. It offers a variety of shopping options, including branded stores, restaurants, and a multiplex theater.
