KOLLAM

Serene

Kollam, also known as Quilon, is a coastal city located in the southern part of the Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its rich history, beautiful beaches, and backwaters, making it a popular tourist destination.
The city has a long and fascinating history, having been ruled by various dynasties over the centuries. Kollam was a major center of trade during the medieval period and was an important hub for the spice trade.
The city is also home to a number of historic landmarks, including the Thangasseri Lighthouse and the Kollam Clock Tower. Kollam is also known for its beautiful beaches, including the famous Kollam Beach and the picturesque Thirumullavaram Beach.
Visitors can also take a boat ride on the backwaters of Ashtamudi Lake, which is the second-largest lake in Kerala and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Other popular tourist attractions in Kollam include the Mahatma Gandhi Park, the Thangassery Fort, and the Jatayu Earth's Center, which is a unique eco-tourism destination that features a giant sculpture of the mythological bird, Jatayu.
Overall, Kollam offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Kerala.

Major Attractions
  1. Ashtamudi Lake: This vast, scenic lake is one of the major attractions in Kollam and is famous for its backwater cruises, which take visitors through serene waterways lined with coconut trees.
  2. Kollam Beach: This picturesque beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing, with its soft sand and clear blue waters.
  3. Thangassery Lighthouse: This historic lighthouse, built in the 19th century, offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding landscape.
  4. Jatayu Earth's Center: This unique eco-tourism destination is built around a massive sculpture of the mythological bird, Jatayu, and offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, including trekking and rock climbing.
  5. Kollam Clock Tower: This historic clock tower, built in 1944, is a major landmark in Kollam and is a popular spot for tourists and locals alike.
  6. Munroe Island: This tranquil island is located in the backwaters of Kollam and is known for its scenic beauty, traditional fishing villages, and birdwatching opportunities.
  7. Palaruvi Waterfalls: This stunning waterfall, located about 75 kilometers from Kollam, is a popular day trip destination for visitors to the region.
