ALAPPUZHA

Venice of the East

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a city located in the state of Kerala, India. It is situated on the west coast of India, with the Arabian Sea to its west. Alappuzha is known for its picturesque backwaters, houseboats, beaches, and traditional coir industry.

The city is also famous for the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, which takes place on the Punnamada Lake in August. The race is a major tourist attraction and features long, narrow boats known as "snake boats" that are powered by rowers.

Alappuzha is also known for its beach, which is one of the cleanest and most beautiful in Kerala. The beach is lined with palm trees and has several resorts and restaurants offering traditional Kerala cuisine.

The city is also home to several temples, churches, and mosques, including the famous Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple, St. Mary's Forane Church, and the Jama Masjid Mosque. Overall, Alappuzha is a popular tourist destination in Kerala, offering visitors a unique experience of Kerala's culture, history, and natural beauty.

Major Attractions
  1. Backwaters: The backwaters of Alappuzha are a network of lagoons, lakes, and canals that offer a serene and scenic view of the surrounding villages and greenery. You can take a houseboat cruise, canoe ride, or a motorboat ride to explore the backwaters.
  2. Houseboats: Houseboats are a unique feature of Alappuzha, and you can hire them to enjoy a comfortable stay and explore the backwaters. These boats are equipped with all modern amenities and offer an unforgettable experience.
  3. Beaches: Alappuzha has some beautiful beaches like Alappuzha Beach, Marari Beach, and Andhakaranazhi Beach. You can enjoy water sports, sunbathing, and seafood at these beaches.
  4. Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple: This temple is one of the famous temples in Kerala and is known for its beautiful architecture and the daily offering of Ambalappuzha Palpayasam, a sweet milk porridge.
  5. Nehru Trophy Boat Race: This annual event takes place in August and is a major tourist attraction. The boat race features long, narrow boats known as "snake boats" that are powered by rowers.
  6. St. Mary's Forane Church: This church is a popular pilgrimage site and is known for its impressive architecture and rich history.
  7. Coir industry: Alappuzha is known for its traditional coir industry, and you can visit the coir factories to see how coir products like mats, carpets, and ropes are made.
